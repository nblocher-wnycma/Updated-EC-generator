# Existing Conditions Style Profile

This style profile is distilled from reviewed CNMP Existing Conditions examples supplied by the user. It captures structure and tone only. Do not copy farm names, field names, addresses, animal inventories, or site-specific facts from example documents into new outputs unless the user enters those facts for the current farm.

## Structure

1. Identify the evaluated farm and section.
2. Describe what was observed during the evaluation.
3. Tie the observation to animal use, manure handling, feed handling, traffic, drainage, or field conditions.
4. Explain where runoff, sediment, nutrients, manure, leachate, or other agricultural waste could move.
5. Identify the resource concern in practical CNMP language.
6. Mention missing documentation as needing confirmation, rather than guessing.

## Tone

- Professional and conservation-planning focused.
- Written as a completed Existing Conditions section, not a short summary.
- Specific to the current farm and the current section.
- Clear about resource concerns, but not alarmist.
- Uses phrases such as "During the evaluation", "it was observed", "runoff has the potential to", and "this creates a resource concern".

## Guardrails

- Do not invent farm-specific facts.
- Do not reuse names or site details from examples.
- Do not leave placeholders in exported output.
- Use "Unknown" when the user does not know an answer, then warn before export.
